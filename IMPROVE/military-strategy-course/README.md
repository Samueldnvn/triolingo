# The Art of War - Military Strategy Course

## Overview
A comprehensive interactive course on military strategy and maneuvering from ancient times through the Napoleonic era. Presented in Renaissance/Da Vinci style with earth tones, parchment textures, and classical aesthetics.

## Course Structure

### 8 Complete Modules

1. **Foundations of Military Strategy** (50+ problems)
   - Nature and purpose of warfare
   - Sun Tzu and The Art of War
   - Thucydides and the Peloponnesian War
   - Enduring strategic principles

2. **Greek and Roman Warfare** (70+ problems)
   - The Greek phalanx
   - Roman manipular and legional systems
   - Roman siegecraft and engineering
   - Key classical battles

3. **Medieval Warfare** (60+ problems)
   - Feudal military organization
   - Castles and siege warfare
   - Knights and heavy cavalry
   - Rise of professional infantry

4. **The Gunpowder Revolution** (60+ problems)
   - Early firearms and cannon
   - Impact on fortifications
   - Spanish tercio formation
   - Transformation of military systems

5. **Renaissance Warfare** (70+ problems)
   - Pike and shot tactics
   - Machiavelli on warfare
   - Dutch military reforms
   - Thirty Years War innovations

6. **The Age of Reason and Linear Tactics** (50+ problems)
   - Linear formations
   - Frederick the Great's oblique order
   - War of Austrian Succession
   - Seven Years War

7. **Napoleonic Warfare** (80+ problems)
   - Corps d'armee system
   - Grand tactics and maneuver
   - Austerlitz, Jena, Russian campaign
   - Waterloo and final defeat

8. **Naval Warfare and Strategic Synthesis** (50+ problems)
   - Ancient naval warfare
   - Age of sail and line of battle
   - Nelsonian tactics
   - Strategic synthesis of enduring principles

### Comprehensive Final Examination
- 25 questions covering all modules
- 120 minutes duration
- 70% passing threshold
- 90% mastery level

## Strategic Coverage

The course covers EVERY major strategy for moving, attacking, defending, positioning, and winning battles used throughout history up to Napoleon:

- **Ancient Strategies**: Phalanx formations, hoplite warfare, Roman manipular system
- **Medieval Strategies**: Castle defense, siege tactics, cavalry dominance
- **Early Modern**: Pike and shot, combined arms, fortress design
- **Renaissance**: Dutch reforms, Swedish innovations, combined arms coordination
- **Linear Era**: Firepower tactics, oblique order, limited war objectives
- **Napoleonic**: Corps d'armee, strategic concentration, operational art
- **Naval Warfare**: Line of battle, breaking the line, sea control
