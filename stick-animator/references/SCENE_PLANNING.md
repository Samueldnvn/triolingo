# Scene Planning Guide

## Planning Your Video

### Step 1: Outline (15 minutes)

Create high-level outline with major story beats:

**Example: French Revolution**
```
1. Introduction (45s)
   - Peasant life misery
   - Estates system unfairness

2. Financial Crisis (60s)
   - France going broke
   - King's lavish spending

3. Estates General (45s)
   - Meeting called
   - Third Estate demands

4. Tennis Court Oath (45s)
   - Rebels pledge change
   - Resistance forms

5. Storming the Bastille (60s)
   - Revolution starts
   - Prison falls

6. Declaration of Rights (45s)
   - New ideals emerge
   - Equality proclaimed

7. Radical Turn (60s)
   - Violence increases
   - King tries to flee

8. Reign of Terror (60s)
   - Guillotine era
   - Fear spreads

9. Fall of Robespierre (45s)
   - Terror ends
   - Justice served

10. Aftermath (45s)
    - Directory struggles
    - Napoleon rises

11. Conclusion (30s)
    - Revolution's legacy
    - What changed
```

**Total:** 10 minutes (600 seconds)

### Step 2: Scene Breakdown (20 minutes)

For each beat, break into individual scenes (3-8 seconds):

**Example: Storming the Bastille - 60 seconds**

```
Scene 1 (4s, RED-INTENSITY):
"Angry crowds gather outside the Bastille fortress in Paris."
Image: Mob with pitchforks, fortress in background

Scene 2 (5s, RED-TENSION):
"They demand weapons and gunpowder stored inside."
Image: Crowds shouting, pointing at fortress

Scene 3 (6s, RED-REVOLUTION):
"Governor de Launay refuses, raising the drawbridge."
Image: Figure on fortress wall, lowering bridge

Scene 4 (5s, RED-ANGER):
"Friction escalates as soldiers and peasants clash."
Image: Chaos, fighting, weapons drawn

Scene 5 (7s, RED-CHAOS):
"People bring down the walls with sheer determination."
Image: Crowds pulling at stone walls, heroic

Scene 6 (5s, RED-HOPE):
"The fortress falls! France's symbol of tyranny is destroyed."
Image: Crowds cheering, fortress broken

Scene 7 (6s, HOPE-TRIUMPH):
"This victory sparks revolution across the entire country."
Image: Map of France, multiple revolutionary icons

Scene 8 (5s, BLUE-BEGINNING):
"The path to freedom has just begun."
Image: Dawn breaking, hopeful figure
```

**Total:** 8 scenes, 43 seconds (adjust to 60 by adding/subtracting scenes)

### Step 3: Word Distribution (15 minutes)

Distribute words across scenes:

**Formula:**
- Target: 1500 words for 10 minutes
- Per scene: 10-20 words (3-8 seconds at 150 wpm)
- Total scenes: ~150-180

**Practical approach:**
```
Fast scenes (3-4 sec): 8-10 words
Medium scenes (5-7 sec): 12-17 words
Slow scenes (7-8 sec): 16-20 words
```

**Example distribution:**
```
Total words: 1500
Breakdown:
  - 50 fast scenes @ 9 words = 450 words (30%)
  - 75 medium scenes @ 14 words = 1050 words (70%)
Total scenes: 125 (5% buffer for slow scenes)
```

### Step 4: Mood Sequencing (10 minutes)

Plan mood color transitions:

**General pattern:**
```
Tragic (start) → Tension (builds) → Revolution (action)
→ Terror (dark) → Hope (light) → Resolution (bright)
```

**Example: French Revolution**
```
Scenes 1-20: Gray/Tragic (misery)
Scenes 21-40: Red/Tension (buildup)
Scenes 41-60: Red/Revolution (action)
Scenes 61-80: Red/Chaos (violence)
Scenes 81-100: Black/Terror (guillotine)
Scenes 101-125: Blue/Hope (reform)
Scenes 126-140: Blue/New beginning (aftermath)
Scenes 141-150: Bright (conclusion)
```

**Tips:**
- Don't change mood too frequently (jarring)
- Use mood to signal story beats
- Dark-to-bright arc for happy endings
- Bright-to-dark for tragedies

### Step 5: Action Planning (15 minutes)

Plan what each stick figure is doing:

**For each scene, determine:**
1. **Main character(s)** - Who's on screen?
2. **Action** - What are they doing?
3. **Emotion** - What's their mood/expression?
4. **Context** - What's happening around them?

**Quick notation method:**
```
Scene 1: Peasant (slumped, sad) staring into empty hearth
Scene 2: Peasant family (crouched, desperate) holding empty bread basket
Scene 3: Aristocrat (formal, dismissive) feasting alone
Scene 4: Crowd (angry, shouting) outside Versailles gates
...
```

**Stick figure pose library:**

**Standing poses:**
- Standing straight (formal, confident)
- Slumped (sad, defeated)
- Leaning forward (angry, aggressive)
- Arms up (celebrating, demanding)

**Sitting poses:**
- Huddled (cold, defeated, hiding)
- Crossed legs (relaxed, noble)
- Kneeling (begging, surrendering)

**Action poses:**
- Running (pursuit, fleeing)
- Fighting (conflict, revolution)
- Celebrating (victory, success)

## Scene Templates

### Template 1: Introduce Situation (3-4 scenes)

**Purpose:** Establish the problem
**Pattern:**
```
Scene 1: Establish baseline (what's normal)
Scene 2: Introduce protagonist (who we follow)
Scene 3: Show the problem (what's wrong)
Scene 4: Heighten tension (why it matters)
```

**Example: "Imagine you're a French peasant"**
```
Scene 1: Empty bread basket on table
Scene 2: Peasant looking at basket (sad)
Scene 3: Bread prices skyrocketing (prices in background)
Scene 4: Family starving at dinner (tragic)
```

### Template 2: Show Progression (4-6 scenes)

**Purpose:** Show change over time
**Pattern:**
```
Scene 1: Starting point
Scene 2: First step/change
Scene 3: Progress continues
Scene 4: Momentum builds
Scene 5: Climax of progression
Scene 6: Result achieved
```

**Example: "Revolution spreads"**
```
Scene 1: Paris in revolt
Scene 2: News reaches countryside
Scene 3: Regional uprisings
Scene 4: Multiple cities revolting
Scene 5: National revolution active
Scene 6: France is in revolution
```

### Template 3: Show Conflict (3-5 scenes)

**Purpose:** dramatize clash/struggle
**Pattern:**
```
Scene 1: Setup the conflict (sides form)
Scene 2: Escalation grows
Scene 3: Climax confrontation
Scene 4: Outcome resolves
```

**Example: "Storming the Bastille"**
```
Scene 1: Crowds gather at fortress
Scene 2: Tension rises with shouts
Scene 3: Fighting breaks out
Scene 4: Fortress falls, victory
```

### Template 4: Show Emotion (2-3 scenes)

**Purpose:** Evoke feeling
**Pattern:**
```
Scene 1: Evoke emotion (setup)
Scene 2: Deepen emotion (intensify)
Scene 3: Show response/result
```

**Example: "The Terror spreads fear"**
```
Scene 1: Guillotine looms (dread)
Scene 2: Execution (horror)
Scene 3: People hiding (fear)
```

### Template 5: Show Change (3-4 scenes)

**Purpose:** Show transformation
**Pattern:**
```
Scene 1: Before state (what was)
Scene 2: Transition begins
Scene 3: Change progresses
Scene 4: After state (what is now)
```

**Example: "France changes from monarchy to republic"**
```
Scene 1: King on throne (monarchy)
Scene 2: Power shifts to assembly
Scene 3: Republic proclaimed
Scene 4: New government formed
```

## Scene Distribution Math

### Calculate Scene Count

**Given duration and average scene length:**
```
Total duration (seconds) ÷ avg scene duration (seconds) = total scenes

Example:
600 seconds (10 minutes) ÷ 6 seconds = 100 scenes
```

**Calculate word distribution:**
```
Target words ÷ scenes = words per scene

Example:
1500 words ÷ 100 scenes = 15 words per scene
```

### Adjust for Variety

**Add variety:**
- 30% faster scenes (3-4 sec): 30 scenes @ 10 words = 300 words
- 50% medium scenes (5-7 sec): 50 scenes @ 14 words = 700 words
- 20% slower scenes (7-8 sec): 20 scenes @ 25 words = 500 words

**Total:** 100 scenes, 1500 words

**Verification:**
```
(30 × 4s) + (50 × 6s) + (20 × 7.5s) = 540s → adjust to 600s
```

## Common Planning Mistakes

### ❌ Don't

- Plan too many long scenes (boring)
- Plan too many short scenes (confusing)
- Forget mood transitions (jarring)
- Mix story beats (disconnected)
- Overlook word count (won't hit target)

### ✅ Do This

- Mix scene durations (engaging)
- Plan clear mood arc
- Plan narrative flow
- Calculate total words
- Verify timing before production

## Quick Start Planning

**For immediate use (no worksheet):**

1. **Outline** (3-5 major beats)
2. **Scenes** - 1 scene per 5-6 seconds = ~100 scenes for 10 min
3. **Words** - 12-17 words per scene = ~1500 words total
4. **Mood** - Start tragic, build tension, end hopeful
5. **Images** - Match pose/action to scene description

**Fast planning in 30 minutes:**
- Outline: 5 min
- Distribute scenes: 10 min
- Write script: 15 min (rapid, can refine later)
