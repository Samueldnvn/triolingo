# Stick Animator - Style Guide

## Visual Style

### Stick Figure Characteristics

**Body proportions:**
```
        O  ← Head (circle, 40px radius)
        |
    /---|---\  ← Arms (lines, width 3px)
        |
       / \     ← Legs (lines, width 3px)
```

**Size:** ~200-300px tall in 1280x720 canvas
**Color:** Black lines (#000000)
**Style:** Simple, clean, recognizable poses
**Detail:** Minimal - focus on posture and action

### Stick Figure Poses (Quick Reference)

**Tragic/Suffering:**
- Slumped shoulders
- Head down
- Arms hanging limp
- Knees slightly bent

**Angry/Revolutionary:**
- Arms raised (fist in air)
- Leaning forward
- Jaw set (line across face)
- Dynamic pose (one foot forward)

**Hopeful/Reform:**
- Standing tall
- Arms open or reaching up
- Looking forward/upward
- Confident stance

**Royal/Aristocratic:**
- Rigid, formal pose
- Crown or hat
- One hand on chest (formal)
- Slight upward gaze

**Terror/Violence:**
- Chaos in posture
- Running or crouching
- Wide arms (panic)
- Disheveled appearance

### Background Colors

**Purpose:** Set mood and emotional tone

**Color Palette:**
```
Tragic/Poverty:
  Gray (#5A5A5A) - Basic tragedy
  Dark Gray (#6B6B6B) - Suffering
  Brown (#8B7355) - Poverty

Tension/Revolution:
  Crimson (#DC143C) - Maximum tension
  Firebrick Red (#B22222) - Revolution
  Indian Red (#CD5C5C) - Anger

Hope/Reform:
  Steel Blue (#4682B4) - Hope
  Dodger Blue (#1E90FF) - Reform

Royal/Aristocracy:
  Purple (#800080) - Basic royal
  Dark Orchid (#9932CC) - Aristocracy

Terror/Chaos:
  Almost Black (#2F2F2F) - Terror
  Dark Red (#8B0000) - Chaos
  Very Dark (#1A1A1A) - Death

Enlightenment/Change:
  Gold (#FFD700) - Enlightenment
  Orange (#FFA500) - Ideas
  Forest Green (#228B22) - Growth
  Lime Green (#32CD32) - New beginning
```

**Usage guidelines:**
- Match background to scene mood
- Can use gradient backgrounds (optional)
- Keep high contrast with black stick figures
- Avoid colors that blend with black (very dark)

## Narrative Style

### "Why It Sucks To Be" Format

**Opening (30-45 seconds):**
- Hook: "Imagine you're born in X time..."
- Establish the tragic/stuck situation
- Build empathy

**Body (8-9 minutes):**
- Progression: misery → action → conflict → climax → resolution
- Continuous storytelling
- Vary scene types (character moments, crowd scenes, events)
- Build tension and release

**Closing (15-30 seconds):**
- Resolution: "But then things changed..."
- Reflect on outcomes
- Leave viewer satisfied

### Storytelling Techniques

**First-person perspective:**
- "Imagine you're..." (not "The peasants were...")
- Creates viewer identification
- More engaging

**Conversational tone:**
- Use contractions ("you're", "don't")
- Simple language
- Occasional humor for relief

**Build narrative momentum:**
- Start bad, show conflict, show action, show change
- Vary pacing (some fast scenes, some thoughtful ones)
- Make the story arc clear

## Scene Pacing

### Ideal Scene Durations

**Fast scenes (3-5 seconds):**
- Action shots
- Transitions
- Quick reactions
- High emotion

**Medium scenes (5-7 seconds):**
- Explanations
- Character moments
- Key story points
- Narrative beats

**Slow scenes (7-8 seconds):**
- Deep emotional moments
- Complex explanations
- Important reveals
- Resolutions

### Scene Variety Pattern

```
Example 10-minute breakage (fast/medium/slow mix):

[Fast 3s] [Medium 5s] [Fast 4s] [Medium 6s] [Fast 3s]
[Slow 7s] [Medium 5s] [Fast 4s] [Medium 5s] [Slow 8s]
[Fast 3s] [Medium 6s] [Fast 4s] [Medium 5s] [Slow 7s]
[Fast 3s] [Medium 5s] [Slow 8s] [Medium 5s] [Fast 3s]
```

**Target distribution:**
- 30% fast (3-4s)
- 50% medium (5-7s)
- 20% slow (7-8s)

## Audio/Narration

### Speaking Rate

**Standard:** 150 words per minute
- Conversational pace
- Good for storytelling
- Matches 6-second average scene

**Adjusted rates:**
- Quick scenes (3-4s): 180-200 wpm
- Medium scenes (5-7s): 150-160 wpm
- Slow scenes (7-8s): 130-140 wpm

### Voice Characteristics

**Engaging:**
- Slight variation in tone
- Occasional emphasis
- Natural pauses (within segments)
- Energy and enthusiasm

**Clear:**
- Good pronunciation
- Not rushed
- Enunciates key words
- Avoids mumbling

**Tone:**
- Humor when appropriate
- Serious when needed
- Varied: don't be monotone
- Reflects scene mood

## Image-Text Synchronization

### Critical Rule

**ONE IMAGE = ONE AUDIO SEGMENT**

The image stays on screen for the exact duration of its associated audio segment.

### Timing Precision

**Perfection not required:**
- ±0.5 second variation OK
- Slight drift over 10 minutes acceptable
- Goal: Match mood, not exact words

**Smooth transitions:**
- Avoid jarring scene changes
- Maintain narrative flow
- Keep viewer engagement

## Technical Specifications

### Image Specifications

**Resolution:**
- HD: 1280x720 (16:9, recommended)
- Full HD: 1920x1080 (16:9, high quality)

**Format:**
- PNG (lossless, preferred)
- JPG (acceptable)
- Avoid: GIF (low quality)

**Bit depth:** 24-bit (RGB)

### Audio Specifications

**Format:** MP3 (preferred)
**Sample rate:** 44100 Hz (standard)
**Bitrate:** 128-192 kbps (good balance)

### Video Specifications

**Codec:** H.264 (widely compatible)
**Container:** MP4
**Frame rate:** 30 fps (standard)
**Bitrate:** 3-5 Mbps (good quality, not huge)

## Examples

### Good Example Scene Combination

**Narration (5.2 seconds):**
"Imagine you're starving in the cold French winter, watching nobles feast inside warm castles."

**Image:**
- Background: Gray (tragic)
- Figure: Slumped peasant, shivering
- Add: Dark castle outline in distance

**Why it works:**
- Matches mood (tragic)
- Matches content (starving vs nobles)
- Appropriate duration (5 seconds)
- Clear visual storytelling

### Bad Example

**Narration (5.2 seconds):**
"In 1789, the Estates General convened at Versailles to address France's financial crisis."

**Image:**
- Background: Blue (inappropriate - this is tension)
- Figure: Standing peasant (boring)
- Add: Nothing

**Problems:**
- Wrong mood (should be tension or revolution)
- Boring image (no action/emotion)
- Doesn't tell the story visually
- Viewer may disengage

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Don't Do

- Use same pose for multiple scenes
- Use wrong mood colors
- Include too much detail (distraction)
- Make scenes too long (boring)
- Make scenes too short (confusing)
- Use complex backgrounds (noise)
- Include text on images (clutter)

### ✅ Do This Instead

- Vary poses to match action
- Match background colors to mood
- Keep it simple and clear
- Aim for 3-8 seconds per scene
- Ensure visual storytelling
- Use solid, bold backgrounds
- Let narration carry information
