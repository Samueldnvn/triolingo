HEXAPOD ROBOT ENGINEERING

From Foundations to Mastery: Engineering a Six-Legged Walking Machine

Course Overview

This comprehensive course provides the theoretical foundation and practical knowledge required to engineer a small hexapod robot to perfection. Designed for students who have completed Calculus III, this course covers all essential engineering disciplines needed to understand, design, build, and control a walking hexapod robot.

01
Foundations & Prerequisites
Vector calculus review, mathematical foundations for robotics, coordinate systems
02
Statics & Dynamics
Forces, torques, equilibrium, rigid body dynamics, Newton-Euler formulation
03
Electricity & Magnetism
Circuits, electromagnetic principles, motors, sensors, electromagnetic interference
04
Circuits & Electronics
Analog and digital circuits, microcontrollers, power electronics, motor drivers
05
Materials Science
Material properties, stress and strain, fatigue, material selection for robotics
06
Differential Equations
ODEs and PDEs, linear systems, Laplace transforms, state-space modeling
07
Kinematics
Forward and inverse kinematics, joint space vs task space, workspace analysis
08
Vibrations & Waves
Free and forced vibrations, damping, resonance, modal analysis
09
Control Theory
Linear systems, frequency domain, PID control, stability analysis
10
Fluid Mechanics
Fluid properties, Bernoulli's principle, drag and lift, fluid power systems
11
Thermodynamics
Heat transfer, thermal management, energy conversion, efficiency
12
Hexapod Design & Gait Planning
Leg kinematics, gait patterns, stability analysis, terrain adaptation
13
Review & Assessment
Comprehensive review, final exam, course completion
