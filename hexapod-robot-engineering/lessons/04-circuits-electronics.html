Module Overview

Analog and digital circuits enable hexapod intelligence. Op-amps condition sensor signals. Microcontrollers execute control algorithms. H-bridges drive motors. PWM smoothly varies motor speed. Linear and switching regulators power electronics.

Learning Objectives

  • Design op-amp circuits: inverting, non-inverting, summing, differentiator
  • Understand digital logic, binary, hexadecimal, microcontroller GPIO
  • Implement PWM for motor speed control
  • Design H-bridge motor drivers for bidirectional control
  • Select power regulators: linear vs switching

Op-Amps

Inverting Amplifier

Gain: \(A_v = -R_f/R_{in}.\)

Non-Inverting Amplifier

Gain: \(A_v = 1 + R_f/R_{in}\) (\(\geq 1\)). Input impedance: high (ideal).

Digital Logic & Microcontrollers

Logic gates perform Boolean operations. Bytes have 8 bits. Microcontrollers execute control algorithms. GPIO pins read sensors and control outputs.

Power Electronics

H-bridges reverse motor polarity. PWM varies duty cycle \(\%\) to control speed. Switching regulators more efficient than linear.
