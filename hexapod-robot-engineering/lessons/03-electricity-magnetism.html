Module Overview

Hexapod robots rely heavily on electrical and magnetic components. Electric circuits power motors, sensors, and control systems. Magnetic principles enable motor operation, position sensing, and electromagnetic interference considerations. This module covers the fundamental principles needed to understand and design hexapod electrical systems.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply Ohm's Law and Kirchhoff's laws to circuits
  • Understand DC motor operation: torque, back-EMF, speed control
  • Analyze magnetic fields, forces, and flux
  • Apply Faraday's Law for electromagnetic induction
  • Understand inductors and their role in circuits

Section 3.1: Electric Circuits

Current (I):

Flow of electric charge through a conductor. Measured in amperes (A). \(I = dq/dt\).

Voltage (V):

Electric potential difference between two points. Measured in volts (V).

Ohm's Law

$$V = IR$$

Where \(V\) is voltage across resistor (V), \(I\) is current through resistor (A), \(R\) is resistance (\(\Omega\)).

Kirchhoff's Laws

  • KVL (Kirchhoff's Voltage Law): Sum of voltage drops around any closed loop = 0
  • KCL (Kirchhoff's Current Law): Sum of currents at any node = 0

Section 3.2: Magnetic Fields and Forces

Magnetic Field (\(\vec{B}\)):

Region around magnet or current-carrying wire where magnetic forces act. Measured in Tesla (T).

Lorentz Force on Moving Charge

$$\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$

Force on charge \(q\) moving with velocity \(\vec{v}\) in magnetic field \(\vec{B}\). Magnetic force perpendicular to both \(v\) and \(B\) (right-hand rule).

Example: Magnetic Field from Current

Magnetic field at point P due to current element \(I d\vec{L}\):

$$d\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{I d\vec{L} \times \hat{r}}{r^2}$$

Where \(\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T} \cdot \text{m/A}\) is permeability of free space.

Section 3.3: Electromagnetic Induction

Faraday's Law

$$\varepsilon = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$$

Where \(\Phi_B = \int_S \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A}\) is magnetic flux through surface. Changing magnetic flux induces EMF in circuit.

Lenz's Law

Induced current creates magnetic field that opposes change in flux. Conservation of energy.

Inductance (L):

Ratio of magnetic flux linkage to current. Units: Henry (H). Inductance \(L = N^2 \Phi_B / I\) for N-turn coil.

Section 3.4: DC Motors

Motor Torque

$$\tau = K_t I$$

Where \(\tau\) is torque (Nm), \(K_t\) is motor torque constant (Nm/A), and \(I\) is current (A).

Back-EMF

When motor rotates, windings cut through magnetic field. By Faraday's Law, back-EMF opposes applied voltage:

$$V_{applied} = IR + K_e \omega$$

Where \(K_e\) is back-EMF constant, \(\omega\) is angular velocity. Faster rotation = more back-EMF.

Motor Dynamics

For a properly designed motor, \(K_t \approx K_e\) numerically (in SI units: Nm/A = V·s/rad).

Example: DC Motor Analysis

DC motor with \(K_t = K_e = 0.1\), \(R = 2\) ohms, no-load speed \(\omega_{no-load} = 100\) rad/s at 24 V. Back-EMF: \(\varepsilon = K_e \omega = 10\) V. Current: \(I = (V - \varepsilon)/R = (24 - 10)/2 = 7\) A. Torque: \(\tau = K_t I = 0.7\) Nm.
