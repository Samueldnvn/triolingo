Module Overview

Kinematics determines how joint angles map to end-effector positions (FK) and what joint angles achieve desired positions (IK). Essential for gait planning.

Forward Kinematics

Given \(\vec{\theta}\), compute \(\vec{x}: \vec{x} = f_{FK}(\vec{\theta}).\) Maps joint to task space.

Inverse Kinematics

Given \(\vec{x}_{desired},\), compute \(\vec{\theta}: \vec{\theta} = f_{IK}^{-1}(\vec{x}_{desired}).\) Mapping from task to joint space.

Jacobian

\(\dot{x} = J\dot{\theta}.\) Relates joint velocities to end-effector velocities. Singularity when \(\det(J) = 0.\)
