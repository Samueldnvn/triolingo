                    DETAILED SKILL SHIFTS
                    ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

INCREASING IMPORTANCE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• AI Product Intuition          ▲▲▲▲▲  Understanding AI's realistic
                                         capabilities and limitations

• Prompt Engineering            ▲▲▲▲   Crafting effective AI interactions
                                         and system instructions

• Agent Orchestration           ▲▲▲▲   Coordinating multiple AI systems
                                         and optimizing workflows

• AI Economics                  ▲▲▲▲   Understanding costs, tradeoffs,
                                         and monetization models

• Data Strategy & Moats         ▲▲▲▲   Collecting, protecting, and
                                         leveraging proprietary data

• AI Ethics & Responsibility    ▲▲▲    Navigating bias, privacy, and
                                         societal impact considerations

DECREASING BUT STILL VALUABLE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Traditional Coding Skills     ▲      Still important, but less
                                         differentiating, shifting to
                                         orchestration and review

• Systems Architecture          ▲↑     Evolving—combining AI with
                                         traditional components, AI
                                         infrastructure considerations

• Basic Technical Literacy      ▲      Now table stakes, assumed
                                         baseline for tech founders

STABLE & CRITICAL (AI AUGMENTED):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Product Vision                ▲▲▲▲▲  Now includes AI-native
                                         product thinking

• Go-to-Market                  ▲▲▲▲   AI creates new GTM channels
                                         and strategies

• Team Building                 ▲▲▲▲   Requires AI literacy across
                                         roles, not just AI specialists

• User Understanding            ▲▲▲▲   AI changes user expectations
                                         and behaviors

• Capital Efficiency            ▲▲▲    AI can reduce costs but also
                                         introduces operational expenses

                    FINDING SUCCESS IN 2025+
                    ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The most successful founders and teams:

1. Combine deep product instinct with AI fluency
2. Think in outcomes, not features (AI handles the rest)
3. Build beyond AI capabilities into workflows and trust
4. Develop data strategies that create lasting advantages
5. Maintain learning agility—AI capabilities evolve quarterly
6. Balance technical leverage (using AI tools) with
   understanding (knowing what's happening under the hood)

Differentiation comes from product insight, domain
expertise, data advantage, distribution, and understanding
how to apply AI effectively to real problems.
