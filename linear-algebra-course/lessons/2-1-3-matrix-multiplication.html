Row-by-Column Multiplication
Key Concept

Matrix multiplication combines rows of the first matrix with columns of the second matrix. Inner dimensions must match!

Definition
Matrix Multiplication

If $$A$$ is $$m \times n$$ and $$B$$ is $$n \times p$$, then $$AB$$ is $$m \times p$$

Formula
$$(AB)_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^n a_{ik} b_{kj}$$
Example
2x2 Multiplication
Step 1

$$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}, B = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$$

Step 2

$$AB = \begin{bmatrix} 1\cdot5+2\cdot7 & 1\cdot6+2\cdot8 \\ 3\cdot5+4\cdot7 & 3\cdot6+4\cdot8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 19 & 22 \\ 43 & 50 \end{bmatrix}$$

Practice Problems
