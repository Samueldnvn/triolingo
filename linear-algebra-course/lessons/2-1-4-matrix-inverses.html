Inverse Matrices
Overview

The inverse of matrix A, denoted $$A^{-1}$$, satisfies $$AA^{-1} = A^{-1}A = I$$. Not all matrices have inverses!

Definition
2x2 Matrix Inverse
Formula
If $$A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}$$, then $$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix}$$ (if $$ad-bc \neq 0$$)
Example
Finding Inverses
Example 1

$$A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$$, then $$ad-bc = 5$$, $$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5}\begin{bmatrix} 4 & -1 \\ -3 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.8 & -0.2 \\ -0.6 & 0.4 \end{bmatrix}$$

Example 2

$$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$$, then $$ad-bc = 0$$, so no inverse exists!

Practice Problems
Problem 1
Does $$\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$$ have an inverse? (yes/no)
