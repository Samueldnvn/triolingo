Three-Dimensional Vectors
Overview

Vectors in 3D space ($$\mathbb{R}^3$$) have three components, representing direction in 3D space. This is essential for 3D graphics, physics, and advanced linear algebra.

Definition
3D Vector
Notation
$$\vec{v} = \langle a, b, c \rangle = \begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix}$$
Definition
Magnitude in 3D
Formula
$$|\vec{v}| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}$$
Definition
Dot Product in 3D
Formula
$$\vec{v} \cdot \vec{w} = v_1 w_1 + v_2 w_2 + v_3 w_3$$
Example
3D Vector Examples
Example 1: Magnitude

$$|\langle 3, 4, 12 \rangle| = \sqrt{9 + 16 + 144} = \sqrt{169} = 13$$

Example 2: Dot Product

$$\langle 1, 2, 3 \rangle \cdot \langle 4, 5, 6 \rangle = 4 + 10 + 18 = 32$$
