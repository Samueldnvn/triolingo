Understanding Dot Product
Overview

The dot product combines two vectors to produce a single scalar (a number). It's fundamental to calculating angles between vectors, projecting vectors, and testing orthogonality (perpendicularity).

Definition
Dot Product
Formula
$$\vec{v} \cdot \vec{w} = v_1 w_1 + v_2 w_2$$

For $$\vec{v} = \langle a, b \rangle$$ and $$\vec{w} = \langle c, d \rangle$$

Example
Dot Product Calculations
Example 1

$$\langle 3, 4 \rangle \cdot \langle 1, 2 \rangle = 3(1) + 4(2) = 3 + 8 = 11$$

Example 2

$$\langle 1, 0 \rangle \cdot \langle 0, 1 \rangle = 1(0) + 0(1) = 0$$

These vectors are orthogonal (perpendicular)!

Important
Orthogonality Test

$$\vec{v} \cdot \vec{w} = 0$$ means vectors are perpendicular

Practice Problems
Problem 1
Calculate $$\langle 2, 3 \rangle \cdot \langle 4, 1 \rangle$$
Problem 2
Are $$\langle 1, 2 \rangle$$ and $$\langle -2, 1 \rangle$$ orthogonal? (yes/no)
Problem 3
Find $$\langle 5, 12 \rangle \cdot \langle 0, 1 \rangle$$
