Applications in Physics
Mechanics
  • Motion: Position $$\vec{r}(t)$$, velocity $$\vec{v} = \vec{r}'$$, acceleration $$\vec{a} = \vec{v}'$$
  • Work: $$W = \int_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}$$ (energy expended by force)
  • Circular Motion: Tangential and centripetal acceleration
Electromagnetism
  • Electric Field: $$\vec{E} = -\nabla V$$ (gradient of potential)
  • Gauss's Law: $$\iint_S \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{S} = \frac{Q}{\epsilon_0}$$ (Divergence Theorem!)
  • Magnetic Fields: Described by curl and Maxwell's equations
Applications in Engineering
Fluid Dynamics
  • Velocity Field: $$\vec{v}(x,y,z,t)$$ describes fluid motion
  • Divergence: $$\nabla \cdot \vec{v}$$ measures compression/expansion
  • Curl: $$\nabla \times \vec{v}$$ measures rotation/circulation
Thermodynamics
  • Heat Flow: Gradient $$\nabla T$$ points hottest to coldest
  • Flux: Rate of heat transfer through surface
Structural Analysis
  • Stress & Strain: Described via tensor fields
  • Center of Mass: Triple integrals compute mass distribution
  • Moments of Inertia: Resistance to rotation
Applications in Mathematics
Probability & Statistics
  • Joint Distributions: Double integrals compute probabilities of multivariate random variables
  • Expected Value: $$\iint xy f(x,y) dx dy$$ for covariance
Differential Equations
  • Heat Equation: $$\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \alpha \nabla^2 u$$ (uses partial derivatives)
  • Wave Equation: $$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2 \nabla^2 u$$
Optimization
  • Lagrange Multipliers: $$\nabla f = \lambda \nabla g$$ find constrained extrema
  • Gradient Descent: Move opposite to $$\nabla f$$ to find minimums
