Module 6: Multiple Integrals

Double, Triple Integrals & Change of Variables | Calculus III

Overview
Integrating Over Higher Dimensions

Multivariable integrals compute volumes, masses, and probabilities over regions. Double integrals give volume under surfaces; triple integrals compute mass density.

Double Integrals
Definition
Double Integral Over Rectangle
Definition
$$\iint_R f(x,y) dA = \lim_{m,n\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^m \sum_{j=1}^n f(x_i^*,y_j^*) \Delta A$$
Fubini's Theorem
Formula
$$\iint_R f(x,y) dA = \int_a^b \int_c^d f(x,y) dy dx = \int_c^d \int_a^b f(x,y) dx dy$$
Interpretation

Volume bounded by surface $$z = f(x,y)$$ above region R in xy-plane

Example
Computing Volume
Problem

Volume under $$z = 4 - x - y$$ over unit square [0,1]×[0,1]

Solution

$$V = \int_0^1 \int_0^1 (4 - x - y) dy dx$$

$$= \int_0^1 [4y - xy - \tfrac{1}{2}y^2]_0^1 dx = \int_0^1 (4 - x - \tfrac{1}{2}) dx$$

$$= \int_0^1 (3.5 - x) dx = [3.5x - \tfrac{1}{2}x^2]_0^1 = 3$$

Double Integrals Over General Regions
Type I Region
Formula
$$\iint_D f(x,y) dA = \int_a^b \int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} f(x,y) dy dx$$

Region between curves $$y = g_1(x)$$ and $$y = g_2(x)$$ for x in [a,b]

Type II Region
Formula
$$\iint_D f(x,y) dA = \int_c^d \int_{h_1(y)}^{h_2(y)} f(x,y) dx dy$$
Triple Integrals
Definition
Triple Integral
Formula
$$\iiint_E f(x,y,z) dV = \int_a^b \int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} \int_{h_1(x,y)}^{h_2(x,y)} f(x,y,z) dz dy dx$$
Physical Meaning

If $$f(x,y,z) = \rho(x,y,z)$$ (density), then triple integral gives total mass of solid E

Example
Volume of Box

Box from (0,0,0) to (2,3,4)

$$V = \int_0^2 \int_0^3 \int_0^4 1 dz dy dx = \int_0^2 \int_0^3 4 dy dx = \int_0^2 12 dx = 24$$

Change of Variables & Jacobian
Jacobian Determinant
2D Transformation
$$\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)} = \begin{vmatrix} x_u & x_v \\ y_u & y_v \end{vmatrix} = x_u y_v - x_v y_u$$
Formula
$$\iint_{D_{xy}} f(x,y) dx dy = \iint_{D_{uv}} f(x(u,v),y(u,v)) \left|\frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)}\right| du dv$$
Polar Coordinates

$$x = r\cos\theta, y = r\sin\theta$$

$$dx dy = r dr d\theta$$ (Jacobian = r)

Example
Polar Integral

$$\iint_R \sqrt{x^2+y^2} dA$$ over circle radius a

$$= \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^a r \cdot r dr d\theta = \int_0^{2\pi} [\tfrac{1}{3}r^3]_0^a d\theta = \int_0^{2\pi} \tfrac{a^3}{3} d\theta = \tfrac{2\pi a^3}{3}$$
